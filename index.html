Honest answers

Questions people actually ask

Will it really play all night?
That's the entire reason SleepBlend exists. It runs as an Android foreground service with a wake lock, and it includes a built-in battery-optimization fix with guidance for your specific device brand — the settings that make other sleep apps go silent at 3am.
Is it really just $1.99? What's the catch?
No catch. You pay $1.99 once on Google Play and everything is unlocked forever — every sound, every blend, every feature, plus updates. There is no subscription, no ads, no locked content, and no "premium" tier.
Does it work offline?
Completely. Every sound is on your device, and the app doesn't even declare the Internet permission — airplane mode, no Wi-Fi, middle of nowhere: it all works the same.
What happens to my data?
Nothing, because none is collected. No analytics, no account, no ads, no tracking — and since the app has no internet permission, it's technically incapable of sending anything anywhere. Mic recordings stay in the app's private storage on your phone. Read the full privacy policy.
Do brainwave beats treat insomnia?
No — and we won't claim they do. Brainwave beats are included for relaxation only; they're not a medical treatment. Many people find them a pleasant way to wind down, and because they're synthesized in real time they loop perfectly. Use headphones for binaural beats, or any speaker for isochronic tones.
Is there an iPhone version?
Not yet — SleepBlend is Android-only for now. The all-night reliability engineering is built specifically around how Android handles background audio.

No internet permission. Your data can't leave your phone.

SleepBlend doesn't just promise privacy — it's technically incapable of breaking it. With no internet permission declared, there's no analytics, no accounts, no ads, and no tracking. Mic recordings never leave the device.
